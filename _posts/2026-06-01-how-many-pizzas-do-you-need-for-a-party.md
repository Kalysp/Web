Planning food for an event is one of those things that sounds simple but quickly gets complicated. Too little and guests go home hungry. Too much and you've wasted money. Pizza, especially at a live catering setup, adds another layer — because you're ordering in advance for a live service.

Here's a practical guide to figuring out the right number.

## The Simple Starting Point

A general rule of thumb: **3 medium-sized pizzas serve roughly 10 people** when pizza is the main course.

For live catering events where guests eat over a period of time (as opposed to a sit-down meal), you can adjust slightly based on the event type:

- **Cocktail / standing events:** 2–2.5 pieces per person
- **Sit-down meals where pizza is the main:** 3–4 pieces per person
- **Casual parties and beach gatherings:** 2.5–3 pieces per person
- **Corporate lunches:** 2–3 pieces per person (people tend to eat a little less at work events)

## Factor in Your Guest List

Kids eat less. Teenagers eat more. A mixed crowd at a beach house party will likely eat more than a corporate lunch crowd. Think about:

- **Age range:** Younger guests tend to eat more
- **Time of day:** Lunch events see slightly less consumption than evening events
- **Other food on offer:** If there are other dishes, pizza consumption goes down
- **Duration of service:** A 2-hour live setup will have guests returning multiple times

At Kaly's, our minimum is 20 pizzas per booking — which comfortably serves around 60–70 guests as part of an event with a live counter running.

## Live Pizza Catering is Different

With a live counter, guests don't take a slice and walk off. They come back. The experience of watching a pizza being made fresh in front of them is part of the fun — which means consumption tends to be slightly higher than a pre-made pizza order.

That's actually a good thing. It means guests are enjoying themselves. But it also means you should plan for 10–15% more than your estimate.

## Our Recommendation

When in doubt, go slightly higher. A few extra pizzas at a live event means your guests don't have to wait too long between rounds. Running out of pizza mid-event is much worse than having a little extra.

If your event is over 70 pizzas, we'd recommend a conversation — setup, team size, and timing all shift at that scale.
